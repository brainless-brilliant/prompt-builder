Prompt Engineering FAQ
General Questions
What makes a good AI prompt?

A good AI prompt is clear, specific, and provides enough context for the AI to understand exactly what you need. It defines the role the AI should take, what task you need completed, and how the output should be formatted.

Why is defining a role important?

Defining a role gives the AI a specific perspective to adopt, which influences its tone, vocabulary, and approach. More specific roles typically yield more tailored responses.

How specific should my task instructions be?

Very specific. Vague instructions lead to vague outputs. The more details you provide about what you want, the better the AI can meet your expectations.

How does tool selection affect the prompt?

Different AI tools have different conventions. Claude Code prefers directive-style prompts, Codex CLI wants terse code-focused instructions, and ChatGPT works best with conversational framing. This builder automatically optimizes the prompt structure for your chosen tool.

Tips for Better Prompts
  1. Be specific about format: If you want a table, specify the columns.
  2. Define your audience clearly: "Senior executives with financial background" is better than "business people."
  3. Use the reasoning field: Asking the AI to show its work leads to more thoughtful responses.
  4. Start with more constraints than fewer: You can always loosen constraints later.
  5. Iterative refinement works best: Use initial output to refine your prompt further.
